## Core AI Prompting Patterns for Leadership

### 1. Strategic Purpose Alignment

```
Review our organizational strategy through the SOLID.AI lens:

Current state:
- Mission: [COMPANY MISSION]
- Strategic priorities: [LIST TOP 3-5]
- AI initiatives: [CURRENT AI USAGE]

Analyze:
1. How well do our AI investments align with our mission?
2. Are we using AI to amplify human potential or replace it?
3. What ethical risks exist in our current approach?
4. Where are we creating vs. extracting value?
5. How do we measure success beyond financial metrics?

Provide alignment assessment and recommendations.
```

**Use when:** Strategic planning or quarterly reviews

---

### 2. Organizational Design

```
Design an AI-native organizational structure for [COMPANY/DIVISION]:

Context:
- Size: [HEADCOUNT]
- Industry: [SECTOR]
- Maturity: [STARTUP / GROWTH / ENTERPRISE]
- Current structure: [DESCRIBE]

Apply SOLID.AI principles:
1. **Squad Formation:** Purpose-driven autonomous teams
2. **Pool Organization:** Shared capability groups
3. **AI Agent Roles:** Where automation creates value
4. **Governance Circles:** Oversight and ethics structures
5. **Communication Flows:** Information and decision pathways
6. **Adaptive Mechanisms:** How org evolves with learning

Generate an organizational topology with rationale.
```

**Use when:** Restructuring or scaling the organization

---

### 3. Investment Prioritization

```
Prioritize these investment opportunities using SOLID.AI criteria:

[LIST OPPORTUNITIES: products, capabilities, markets, etc.]

Evaluate each on:
1. **Purpose Alignment:** Mission and values fit
2. **Human Value:** Real needs addressed, not just efficiency
3. **AI Readiness:** Data and capability maturity
4. **Ethical Risk:** Potential harms and mitigation costs
5. **Learning Potential:** Strategic insights gained
6. **Organizational Capacity:** Team readiness and load
7. **Competitive Position:** Market timing and differentiation

Provide ranked recommendations with risk assessment.
```

**Use when:** Allocating budget or resources

---

### 4. Culture and Values

```
Assess our organizational culture for AI-native readiness:

Current culture indicators:
- Decision-making style: [CENTRALIZED / DISTRIBUTED]
- Failure tolerance: [HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW]
- Learning practices: [DESCRIBE]
- AI perception: [TOOL / THREAT / TEAMMATE]

Evaluate alignment with SOLID.AI principles:
1. **Purpose-Led Decisions:** Do values guide choices?
2. **Continuous Learning:** Is failure seen as growth?
3. **Human-AI Symbiosis:** Are AI and humans collaborating?
4. **Intelligent Decentralization:** Are teams empowered?
5. **Ethical Automation:** Is transparency valued?

Identify culture gaps and transformation steps.
```

**Use when:** Driving cultural transformation

---

### 5. Governance Framework Design

```
Design a governance framework for AI usage in our organization:

Context:
- Industry regulations: [COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS]
- Risk tolerance: [CONSERVATIVE / MODERATE / AGGRESSIVE]
- AI maturity: [EARLY / GROWING / ADVANCED]

Define:
1. **Decision Rights:** Who approves what level of AI deployment
2. **Ethics Review:** When and how ethical assessment happens
3. **Oversight Mechanisms:** Monitoring and accountability structures
4. **Escalation Paths:** How issues are raised and resolved
5. **Transparency Standards:** What we disclose internally and externally
6. **Audit and Compliance:** How we verify responsible AI usage
7. **Continuous Improvement:** How governance evolves

Generate a governance charter.
```

**Use when:** Establishing or updating AI governance

---

### 6. Stakeholder Communication

```
Craft a message to [STAKEHOLDER GROUP: board, investors, employees, customers] about our AI strategy:

Key points to convey:
- [POINT 1]
- [POINT 2]
- [POINT 3]

Frame using SOLID.AI storytelling:
1. **Purpose:** Why we're pursuing this (mission alignment)
2. **Approach:** How we're different (human-AI symbiosis)
3. **Safeguards:** How we manage risks (ethics, transparency)
4. **Value:** What stakeholders gain (outcomes, not just features)
5. **Learning:** How we adapt and improve (continuous evolution)
6. **Call to Action:** What we need from them (support, feedback)

Draft a compelling message with supporting data.
```

**Use when:** Communicating major AI initiatives

---

### 7. Talent Strategy

```
Develop a talent strategy for an AI-native organization:

Current state:
- Team composition: [ROLES AND SKILLS]
- AI capability gaps: [AREAS NEEDING GROWTH]
- Retention challenges: [KEY CONCERNS]

Design for:
1. **Roles Evolution:** How jobs change with AI augmentation
2. **Hiring Criteria:** Skills and mindsets for AI collaboration
3. **Learning & Development:** Upskilling for human-AI symbiosis
4. **Retention:** Keeping talent engaged in AI-enabled work
5. **Diversity & Inclusion:** Ensuring ethical AI through diverse teams
6. **Career Paths:** Growth in an AI-augmented organization

Generate a talent roadmap.
```

**Use when:** Workforce planning or HR strategy

---

### 8. Risk Assessment

```
Assess strategic risks of our AI initiatives:

Current AI usage:
- [INITIATIVE 1]
- [INITIATIVE 2]
- [INITIATIVE 3]

Evaluate risks across:
1. **Ethical:** Bias, fairness, transparency, consent
2. **Operational:** Reliability, security, performance
3. **Reputational:** Public perception, trust, brand
4. **Regulatory:** Compliance, legal exposure, policy changes
5. **Competitive:** Being outpaced or disrupted
6. **Financial:** ROI, cost overruns, opportunity cost
7. **Organizational:** Change fatigue, skill gaps, culture mismatch

Provide risk matrix and mitigation priorities.
```

**Use when:** Strategic risk planning or board reporting

---

### 9. Metrics and OKRs

```
Define organizational metrics for AI-native operations:

Levels:
1. **Company-Level OKRs:** Strategic objectives
2. **Squad-Level Metrics:** Team health and impact
3. **AI Agent Performance:** Automation effectiveness
4. **Ethical Indicators:** Responsible AI scorecard
5. **Learning Velocity:** How fast we improve

For each level, specify:
- Objective (the "what" and "why")
- Key Results (measurable outcomes)
- Leading indicators (early signals)
- Lagging indicators (final outcomes)
- Review cadence (when we assess)

Generate a balanced metrics framework.
```

**Use when:** Setting goals or measuring success

---

### 10. Change Management

```
Plan a change management initiative for [TRANSFORMATION]:

Context:
- Current state: [DESCRIBE]
- Desired state: [TARGET OUTCOME]
- Affected population: [WHO'S IMPACTED]
- Timeline: [DURATION]

Design change program:
1. **Vision & Purpose:** Compelling story of why this matters
2. **Stakeholder Mapping:** Who needs to be enrolled and how
3. **Communication Plan:** Messages, channels, cadence
4. **Training & Support:** How people develop new capabilities
5. **Quick Wins:** Early successes to build momentum
6. **Resistance Handling:** Anticipated objections and responses
7. **Measurement:** How we know change is taking hold
8. **Sustainability:** How new ways become "how we work"

Generate a change roadmap.
```

**Use when:** Leading organizational transformation

## SOLID.AI Leadership Mindset

✅ **Do:**
- Lead with purpose and values, not just profit
- Model human-AI collaboration personally
- Create psychological safety for experimentation
- Make ethical considerations visible in decisions
- Empower teams with autonomy and clear direction
- Measure what matters, including qualitative impact
- Learn publicly from failures
- Invest in organizational learning capacity

❌ **Avoid:**
- "AI-first" thinking that sidelines humans
- Short-term optimization over long-term value
- Command-and-control in adaptive organizations
- Metrics that incentivize gaming or harm
- Ignoring ethical risks until crisis
- Centralizing decisions that should be distributed
- Technology solutions to culture problems

## Leadership Rituals for AI-Native Orgs

### Weekly
- **Squad Check-ins:** Understand frontline challenges and learnings
- **AI Performance Review:** Monitor agent effectiveness and issues
- **Ethics Pulse:** Surface ethical concerns early

### Monthly
- **Learning Retrospectives:** What did we discover? How do we adapt?
- **Purpose Alignment:** Are initiatives serving mission?
- **Metrics Review:** Balanced scorecard across all dimensions

### Quarterly
- **Strategy Refresh:** Adjust based on learning and market
- **Organizational Health:** Culture, engagement, capacity assessment
- **Governance Audit:** Verify responsible AI practices

### Annually
- **Vision Setting:** Update multi-year direction
- **Talent Planning:** Skills, roles, development needs
- **External Engagement:** Share learnings, contribute to community

## Conversation Starters with Your Team

**On Purpose:**
"If we could only work on one thing this quarter, what would create the most human value?"

**On AI:**
"Where is AI augmenting our team's capabilities vs. creating busy work or black boxes?"

**On Ethics:**
"What keeps you up at night about how we're using AI?"

**On Learning:**
"What did we learn from our recent failures? How are we changing because of it?"

**On Culture:**
"Do people feel safe to experiment, fail, and speak up?"
